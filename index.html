My Bio

Hi, I'm Ryan. I'm a current freshman Computer Science major at the University of Maryland and a recent graduate of Pennsbury High School in Fairless Hills, PA. I am self-taught in HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript, and jQuery. I'm also self-taught in Java with 3 years of formal education as well. In ninth grade, I began to get seriously interested in programming and spent countless hours researching new subjects and learning how to practically implement them in my code. I've been teaching myself coding over the past 4 years and have made multiple projects and webpages (including this one!). I certainly plan to broaden my coding horizons over the next four years at UMD.

This was my first major project and took around a year and a half to complete. Castle Attack

A fun, addictive single-player defense game. You must spawn soldiers and fire projectiles to try to destroy the enemy's castle before he destroys yours! Use gold to purchase upgrades and special items in the shop. In case you want to pick up where you left off, the game features save and load capabilities. Working on this project taught me about both writing to and reading from files in Java along with creating my own custom interface.

SolarSim

SolarSim is a web application I created to gain experience with JavaScript, jQuery, and three.js, a JavaScript library for 3D animation. This solar system simulator features scaled distances and orbits, as well as realistic textures. This project was a great way to combine two interests of mine: astronomy and programming.

This is one of my favorite creations because it was my first taste of game programming.
